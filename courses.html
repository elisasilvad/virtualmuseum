The project

The pandemic involved humanity in its entirety.
Like any macro-event, it has affected each of us differently: who fell ill, who lost a loved one, who their jobs, who put themselves on the front line felt it differently.
Reflecting the prismatic nature of this event is the role of this museum.

History VS Memory

It's often difficult, as humans, to distinguish between what really happened and what we perceived from our individual perspective. This particular period of time changed us a lot, and we have to remember what we thought and what we did without letting Memory transform it again.



Virtual Museum

A virtual museum can be able to display and help us process these memories without fouling them. Controlled materials of creators, witnesses and common individuals. It is also open, inclusive and useful for ourselves in the future.



Collective Memory

Narrate a collective memory while it is still strong in our minds. Do not let time consume and modify our experience. Let it rest to be reckoned in the future, and listen to our past selves to remember.



Build together

Collaboration with the public and open possibility to submit material to be displayed. The material should also be preserved through metadata (XML) in order to be fruible in the long term.

About IMOW

IN MY OWN WORDS is an original project about a virtual museum dedicated to the global-wide event of the COVID-19 emergency situtation. IMOW is aimed at collecting in a single virtual place the memory of this time: celebrating its collective aspect as well as preserving its single features.
Because it's the first time no one is a simple visitor.
